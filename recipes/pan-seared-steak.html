Pan-seared Steak

Description

A perfectly seared steak is a lovely treat that can be enjoyed any week of the night. What's even better is that cooking it just right is not as hard as you may think. This recipe will give you step-by-step instructions for perfecting a garlic-butter basted steak cooked to your liking.

Ingredients

  • 8-16oz New York Strip or Ribeye
  • canola oil
  • 2 tablespoons of unsalted butter
  • 2 cloves of peeled garlic
  • fresh thyme
  • kosher salt
  • freshly cracked black pepper

Steps

  1. Take your steak out of the fridge about 1 hour before you intend to begin cooking. Season both sides liberally with kosher salt and freshly cracked black pepper. Allow the steak to sit out on a cutting board for the rest of the hour so it reaches room temperature.
  2. Once the steak has sat out for an hour, heat a large cast iron skillet over medium-high heat. It should preheat for about 3-5 minutes, until it begins to smoke.
  3. Once the skillet is done pre-heating, add about 2 tablespoons of canola oil to the skillet. Tilt the skillet around to spread out the oil.
  4. Place the steak in the skillet by laying it away from you so the oil doesn't hit you if it splatters.
  5. Allow the steak to cook for 2 and a half minutes without touching or moving it so it develops a proper crust.
  6. After 2 and a half minutes have passed, flip the steak over.
  7. After 1 minute, crush both cloves of garlic. Add the garlic, thyme, and butter to the skillet.
  8. Use a spoon to mix around the garlic, thyme, and butter. Tilt the pan and use the spoon to baste the steak with the butter.
  9. Continue basting until the second side of the steak has cooked a total 3 minutes for a medium-rare steak.
  10. Remove the steak from the pan and place it on a clean cutting board. Cover the steak loosely with foil.
  11. Allow the steak to rest for at leat 5 minutes but no more than 10.
  12. Once the steak has finished resting, you are ready to serve. Enjoy!
